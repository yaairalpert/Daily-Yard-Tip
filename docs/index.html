Heat spike coming — adjust watering and mowing height

  1. Water deeply (about 1 inch) before 9am, not in the evening — overnight moisture on hot leaves invites fungus.
  2. Raise the mower deck a notch — taller grass shades its own roots and holds moisture better in a heat spike.
  3. Check new/young plantings and container plants twice a day — they dry out fastest and show stress first.
  4. Avoid mowing during the hottest part of the day — it stresses grass that's already fighting heat.
Why: Grass and young plants lose far more moisture than they can take up once highs push past 90°F, so watering timing and mower height matter more than usual.
